Volatility Analytics

Returns are normalized based on the prior market implied volatility reading. For example, if VIX = 20.0 on a particular day, then the implied standard deviation of monthly returns = 20/100/sqrt(12) ~ 5.8%. Then, observe the lowest SPX price over the ensuing 21 trading days, calculate the log return, and divide by 5.8% to arrive at the worst 1 month standard deviation return.
